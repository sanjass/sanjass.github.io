I am Sanja Simonovikj and I am currently pursuing my Master of Engineering (MEng) degree in Computer Science and Engineering with a concentration in AI at MIT with the class of 2021. I graduated with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science and Engineering at MIT with the class of 2020.

My coursework and projects have a focus on Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, Probability, Inference and related.

Experience

At MIT CSAIL, I have the honor to work with the ALFA research group first as an Undergraduate Researcher working on tree-structured program representations and their usage in Deep Learning tasks and now as a graduate Research Assistant.

Until now I've had the opportunity to intern in both small and large companies, in US and abroad. I love to combine my passion for travelling with my desire to learn, grow and explore.

During the summer of 2020 I was a graduate Research Assistant at the Improbable AI group at CSAIL, under supervision of professor Pulkit Agrawal working on a project with an overarching goal to investigate and solve the problem of neural networks learning shortcuts/biases in a dataset through having diverse hypotheses.

This January (2020) I was an extern at IBM Research in Cambridge, MA working on reasoning about Visual Intuitive Physics in the Machine Common Sense group.

In the summer of 2019 I was interning at Qualcomm in San Diego, CA with the Secure Systems Group.

I spent the summer of 2017 in Chihuahua, Mexico working at BlackLabs, and the summer of 2018 at Shell in Bangalore, India working on NLP tasks.

For other relevant experience please refer to my resume.

Open to opportunities

I am open to entry-level full-time opportunities in US or Canada starting from summer 2021. I am also open to short full-time externships during January 2021.

About me

Outside of class I like catching up with my old friends, hanging out with my current ones and just enjoying the simple things in life. I believe in the importance of work-life balance and I value honesty, reliability, kindness and inclusion.
